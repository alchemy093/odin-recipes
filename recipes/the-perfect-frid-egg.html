The Perfect Fried Egg

The Perfect Fried Egg Home

The humble fried egg is, from the perspective of the ingredients and tools required, about as simple as it gets for a recipe. In terms of the execution required, however, it is a very active recipe that requires a cook to be perceptive and alert. The difference between a perfect, over medium egg where the yolk has thickened but still runs when pierced and a scrambled interior or a watery mess is literal seconds when we are frying in a hot pan. Practice often, and be mindful of what you are seeing, hearing, and smelling as you do so.

Ingredients
  • Egg
  • Oil
  • Seasoning - salt, pepper, you can get crazy and add more if you want, but try adding one thing at a time
  • a cast iron skillet
  • a thoughtful cook

  1. Put some olive oil in your skillet - a bit more than you think you'll need. We will be spooning some of it to cook the top of the egg later.
  2. Have your seasoning ready right next to the pan. Crack your egg(s) in the pan, and season them right away.
  3. As the bottom starts to cook, tilt your (hopefully small, 6-8 inch) skillet so that some of the oil pools on one side. Use a spoon to spoon some of the oil on top of the egg and cook the white on top along with the yolk.
  4. Remove when white is no longer goopy and the yolk is just right. Enjoy!
